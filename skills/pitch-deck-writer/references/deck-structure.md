# Pitch Deck Structure Reference

This reference is used by the `pitch-deck-writer` skill.

## Investor Deck Structure (Series A and earlier)

Recommended: **10–14 slides**. Each slide = one idea, one bold headline, max 3 bullets.

| Slide | Title             | What it answers                                 |
| ----- | ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Executive Summary | "What is this company in one paragraph?"        |
| 2     | Problem           | "What painful problem exists?"                  |
| 3     | Solution          | "How do you solve it uniquely?"                 |
| 4     | Product           | "What does it actually look like?"              |
| 5     | Market Size       | "How big is the opportunity?" (TAM → SAM → SOM) |
| 6     | Business Model    | "How do you make money?"                        |
| 7     | Traction          | "What proof do you have it works?"              |
| 8     | Go-to-Market      | "How will you grow?"                            |
| 9     | Competition       | "What's the landscape and your moat?"           |
| 10    | Team              | "Why you?"                                      |
| 11    | Financials        | "What are your projections and burn?"           |
| 12    | The Ask           | "How much, for what milestones?"                |

## Slide-by-Slide Writing Guide

### Slide 1 — Executive Summary

One paragraph. State: what you do, for whom, traction signal, and the ask.

> "Acme helps mid-market logistics companies reduce fuel spend by 22% using AI route optimisation. We have $1.2M ARR growing 15% MoM. Raising $4M to double the sales team."

### Slide 2 — Problem

Real customer evidence preferred. Quantify the pain.

- ✅ "Finance teams spend 8 hours/week reconciling spreadsheets manually"
- ❌ "Businesses struggle with inefficiency"

### Slide 3 — Solution

One sentence saying what you do. Then differentiation.
Avoid: "We leverage AI/ML to disrupt…" — say what the product actually does.

### Slide 5 — Market Size

Use bottom-up sizing when possible:

- TAM: total global market
- SAM: the segment you can reach
- SOM: realistic 3-year target share (back it up with a growth assumption)

### Slide 7 — Traction

Lead with the best number. Then secondary signals.
Examples: ARR, MoM growth rate, logo count, NPS, retention, partnerships.

> "$850K ARR · 120% net revenue retention · 3 enterprise pilots signed Q1 2026"

### Slide 10 — Team

Investors bet on people first. Include:

- Relevant domain expertise
- Prior exits or notable companies
- Why **this team** for **this problem**

### Slide 12 — The Ask

Be specific:

- Amount raising
- Instrument (SAFE, priced round)
- Use of funds (3–5 line items, %)
- 18-month milestones the round funds

## Internal / Strategy Deck Structure

For board or exec presentations:

| Slide | Content                                    |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | The Ask (recommendation + decision needed) |
| 2     | Situation / Context                        |
| 3     | Key findings (data-backed)                 |
| 4     | Options considered                         |
| 5     | Recommended option + rationale             |
| 6     | Risks and mitigations                      |
| 7     | Next steps + owners + dates                |

**Rule:** Lead with the ask, not the analysis. Executives decide first, then dive into data if needed.

---

## Headline Writing Rules

| Principle                        | Example                                                        |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| State the insight, not the label | "Revenue grew 3× YoY" not "Revenue Chart"                      |
| Use numbers                      | "22% cost reduction in 90 days"                                |
| Be specific                      | "Two enterprise deals worth $400K total" not "Strong pipeline" |
| Present tense, active voice      | "We own 12% of the SMB segment"                                |

---

## Common Investor Deck Mistakes

| Mistake                     | Fix                                              |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Too many slides (20+)       | Cut to 12; move detail to appendix               |
| Vague market sizing         | Show bottom-up calculation with assumptions      |
| No traction slide           | Include any signal — even pilots, LOIs, waitlist |
| Weak team slide             | Show domain expertise + why you specifically     |
| Missing the ask             | State amount, instrument, milestones clearly     |
| Acronyms without definition | Spell it out on first use                        |
