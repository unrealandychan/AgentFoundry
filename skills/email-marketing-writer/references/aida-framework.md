# AIDA Framework Reference

This reference is used by the `email-marketing-writer` skill.

## What is AIDA?

**AIDA** is the foundational copywriting framework for marketing emails:

| Stage         | Goal                             | Question it answers       |
| ------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **A**ttention | Stop the scroll / open the email | "Why should I read this?" |
| **I**nterest  | Build relevance and curiosity    | "What's in this for me?"  |
| **D**esire    | Make them want the outcome       | "Why do I need this now?" |
| **A**ction    | Drive a specific next step       | "What do I do next?"      |

---

## Email Anatomy

```
From name:    [Brand] or [Person at Brand] — avoid "noreply@"
Subject:      ≤50 chars · curiosity or urgency · no spam words
Preview text: Complements subject (never repeats it) · ≤90 chars

--- body ---
Greeting:    "Hi [First Name]," (personalised) or "Hey there,"

A — Attention (1-2 sentences)
  Hook: bold statement, surprising stat, or empathetic problem acknowledgement

I — Interest (2-4 sentences)
  Expand on the problem or opportunity; build relevance to this reader

D — Desire (2-4 sentences)
  Paint the after-state; social proof, benefit language, specifics

A — Action (1-2 sentences + button)
  One CTA. Active verb. Clear destination.

Signature:  [Name], [Title] at [Brand]
Unsubscribe link (required)
```

## Subject Line Rules

### Must-Dos

- ≤ 50 characters (≤40 for mobile)
- Active verb or intriguing question
- Promise something specific: "3 ways to…", "Your account needs attention", "We saved your cart"

### Must-Avoids (Spam Triggers)

| Avoid           | Alternative                       |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- |
| FREE!!!         | "On us:" or "Complimentary"       |
| 100% guaranteed | Specific proof or stat            |
| Act now!        | "Ends Friday" (specific deadline) |
| Click here      | "[Do specific thing]"             |
| ALL CAPS        | Capitalise only the first word    |

### The 3-Variant Rule

Always write 3 subject lines per email using different angles:

1. **Curiosity** — "The mistake most teams make before Q4"
2. **Benefit** — "Ship 2× faster with this workflow"
3. **Urgency/FOMO** — "This closes in 48 hours"

## Preview Text Patterns

Preview text is 40–90 chars; it's the second hook.

| Subject                       | Preview                                                  |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Your trial ends tomorrow"    | "Here's what happens to your data (and how to keep it)." |
| "The mistake most teams make" | "Hint: it costs them 10+ hours a week."                  |
| "We built something new"      | "Only for customers who've been with us > 6 months."     |

Never use default filler: "View in browser" or "If you can't see this email…"

---

## CTA Best Practices

| Do                      | Don't                |
| ----------------------- | -------------------- |
| "Start your free trial" | "Click here"         |
| "See the full report"   | "Learn more"         |
| "Claim your 30% off"    | "Submit"             |
| "Book a 15-min demo"    | "Contact us"         |
| One CTA per email       | Three competing CTAs |

---

## Scannability Checklist

- [ ] Paragraphs ≤ 3 sentences
- [ ] Key phrases **bolded** (max 2–3 per email)
- [ ] Bullet lists for features or steps
- [ ] Single-column layout for mobile
- [ ] CTA button is above the fold on mobile (≤600px viewport)
- [ ] Total word count: 100–300 words for promotional; 300–600 for newsletters
